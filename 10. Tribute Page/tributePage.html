Gabriel García Marquez

The man who writed Colombia.

Here's a time line of Gabriel's life:

  • 1927 Born in Aracataca, Colombia
  • 1940 Finished secondary Education in San José school, where he published his first poems in the Juventud educational magazine.
  • 1947 Published his first story in El Espectador newspaper: "The Third Resignation".
  • 1950 Desisted being a lawyer in order to focus on journalism. Moves to Barranquilla for working to El Heraldo newspaper.
  • 1955-1962 Published his first novels in the respective order: "Leaf Storm", "No One Writes to the Colonel", "In Evil Hour" and "Big Mama's Funeral"
  • 1967 Published his major novel: "One Hundred Years of Solitude", being sold in a week 8.000 copies. It was translated to more than twenty-four languages and won four international rewards.
  • 1974-1980 Found the Alternativa magazine, marking one important moment in the history of Journalism in Colombia.
  • 1982 Won the International Nobel Prize for Literature.
  • 1985-1992 Published the novels: "Love in the Time of Cholera", "Clandestine in Chile", and "The General in His Labyrinth".
  • 1996 Published the non-fiction story "News of a Kidnapping", where he combines the testimonial orientation of Journalism and his own narrative style.
  • 2002-2004 Published his memories book: "Living to Tell the Tale".
  • 2007 In Aracataca, makes a tribute to the Colombian government.
  • 2014 Dies at the age of 87.

"A new and sweeping utopia of life, where no one will be able to decide for others how they die, where love will prove true and happiness be possible, and where the races condemned to one hundred years of solitude will have, at last and forever, a second opportunity on earth."

--Nobel Lecture, Gabriel García Marquez
